ECU Module Opening Station

Veoneer Internship

Objective:

During my internship, I was tasked with creating a CNC module opening station for testing points on the PCB of a new ECU product. While ECU modules are typically easy to open, a new module was being introduced which was fully sealed to protect against water. This sealant was quite tough and significantly increased the difficulty of this simple task.


Problem:

At the time this project took place, the CNC machine was booked for brake module troubleshooting. This meant that the ECU module opening would have to be scheduled around the brake schedule, and the operational time would have to be minimized. An issue was that the ECU module would require a separate fixture, meaning a fixture changeover would have to take place.


Initial Approach:

I initially intended to cut the metal baseplate of the module using the CNC machine, but faced challenges with metal shavings contaminating the PCB, necessitating three cuts along the plastic housing for base plate removal, and scheduling difficulties with the CNC machine. This required a longer router bit, which contributed to a longer changeover as the same bit could no longer be used for the ECU and brake modules. This changeover lead time was minimized by creating technical and standard work instructions, and modifying the fixture to slot into the brake module fixture. Furthermore, the single fixture was designed to hold all three orientations of the ECU module to avoid unnecessary changeovers. The longer bit also introduced a higher risk of breaking the bit, so the G-Code was modified to perform multi-sweep cuts to prevent bit breakage.



Final CNC Station Design:

Having to change the bit still introduced some lag before the station could be used, and introduced delays in the brake module schedule. After consulting with the project lead and industrial engineers, I decided to look into solutions that did not require the CNC machine.



Final Design:

Opting for a simple design that utilized a sonic cutter and a putty knife led to significant cost savings, elimination of the changeover process, no more scheduling conflicts, and a process better suited to the task's low frequency. This solution effectively reduced complexity, and lowered energy consumption.



Key Learnings:

This project emphasized the importance of adapting to new skills and software, adopting a holistic perspective towards problem-solving, taking ownership of projects, and being open to alternative solutions.
